Your task in this experiment is to infer, based on the information given to you, information about players in hypothetical tug-of-war games.

In each scenario, you will see the outcomes of a number of such games. The members of each team in a game will be shown in boxes. A crown will appear next to the team that won each game. You may receive additional information about the matches from a commentator, which will appear on the side of the screen. Always take this information to be true.

You will be asked to answer questions about players' strength and laziness. Strength can be thought of as a number. If the sum of the strengths of Team Blue are greater than the sum of the strengths of Team Red, then Team Blue wins. A player's strength remains the same throughout each scenario (but not necessarily across scenarios).

If a player is lazy in a certain game, then he or she only tries half as hard and therefore his or her strength is decreased by half. However, a player can be lazy in one game and then try hard in the next.
